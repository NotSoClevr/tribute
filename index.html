James Earl Jones

A voice that resonated through generations

Timeline of James Earl Jones's Life

  • 1931 - Born in Arkabutla, Mississippi
  • 1955 - Graduated from the University of Michigan with a Bachelor of Arts
  • 1957 - Broadway debut as understudy to Lloyd Richards in The Egghead by Molly Kazan
  • 1958 - Created the role of Edward the butler in sunrise at campobello
  • 1961 - Appeared in an off-broadway production of The Blacks
  • Early to mid 1960's - Acted in various works of Shakespeare's, becoming one of the best known Shakespearean actors of the time
  • 1964 - Film debut in Dr. Strangelove or: How I Learned to Stop Worrying and Love the Bomb
  • 1969 - Won the Tony Award for Best Actor in a Play for his role in The Great White Hope
  • 1970 - First leading film role in the film adaptation of The Great White Hope and became the second African-American male to be nominated for Best Actor
  • 1974 - Co-starred in the film Claudine
  • 1977 - Debut voiceover role as Darth Vader in Star Wars: A New Hope
  • 1988 - Appeared in Coming to America
  • 1990 - Appeared in The Hunt for Red October
  • 1992 - Presented with the National Medal of the Arts
  • 1993 - Appeared in The Sandlot
  • 1994 - Voiced Mufasa in Disney's The Lion King
  • 1998 - Starred in An American Moment
  • 2000-2009 - Guest starred on various TV shows
  • 2010-2022 - Returned to broadway and later retired
  • 2024 - Died at the age of 93

"When I read great literature, great drama, speeches, or sermons, I feel that the human mind has not achieved anything greater than the ability to share feelings and thoughts through language."

-- James Earl Jones
